The Meaning of "Analytics" in Our Data World

In data science, especially in big data field, analytics is defined as a broad term that encompasses the process, technologies, frameworks and algorithms to extract meaningful insights from data.

The choice of the technologies, algorithms, and frameworks for analytics is driven by the analytics goals of the application. There are 3 goals that analytics aims to:

  • To predict something
    Ex: Whether a transaction is fraud or not?
  • To find patterns in the data
    Ex: Finding the top 10 coldest days in the year
  • To find relationships in the data
    Ex: Finding related products on an eCommerce website

    • The National Research Council has done a characterization of computational tasks for massive data analysis.This characterization of computational tasks aims to provide a taxonomy of tasks that have proved to be useful in data analysis and grouping them roughly according to mathematical structure and computational strategy. There are 7 computational tasks for big data analysis which are also called "7 giants". The list of these tasks is shown below:

  • Basis Statistic
  • Generalized N-Body Problems
  • Linear Algebraic Computation
  • Graph-Theoretic Computation
  • Optimization
  • Integration
  • Alignment

    • 4 Types of Analytics

  • Descriptive Analytics
    Descriptive Analytics comprises analyzing past data to present it in an easily interpreted summarized form. This type of analytics aims to answer the question "What was happened?
  • Diagnostic Analytics
    Diagnostic Analytics comprises analysis of past data to diagnose the reasons as to why certain events happened and aims to answer "Why did it happen?"
  • Predictive Analytics
    Predictive Analytics comprises the predicting the occurrence of an event or the likely outcome of an event or forecasting the future values using prediction models and uses prediction models to predict the likely outcome of an event This analytics aims to answer "What is likely to happen?"
  • Prescritve Analytics
    Prescriptive uses multiple prediction models to predict various outcomes and the best course of action for each outcome and aims to answer "What can we do to make it happen?"
